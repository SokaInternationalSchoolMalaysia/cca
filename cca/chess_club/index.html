Get to know Chess Club

Chess Club is a student activity that brings together learners who enjoy strategic board games, including Chinese Chess (Xiangqi) and International Chess.

Sessions are built around friendly matches, paired practice, and small-group challenges where students learn through playing, discussing moves, and reflecting on decisions.

Objectives

  • Develop students’ strategic thinking and decision-making through structured chess play.
  • Strengthen logical reasoning, concentration, and the ability to plan ahead.
  • Encourage positive peer interaction through learning, practising, and improving together.
  • Build a school culture that values discipline, sportsmanship, and thoughtful competition.

Suitable Students & Why You Should Join

Suitable for students from all year groups, including beginners and experienced players. It is ideal for students who enjoy problem-solving, strategy, puzzles, or games that involve planning and analysis.

Students who want to improve focus and patience, or who prefer a quieter, thinking-based activity, will benefit from joining the club. It is also suitable for those who want to learn a new game and gain confidence through gradual improvement.

Chess helps students think clearly under pressure and make decisions with limited time and information. It also teaches how to handle wins and losses with maturity while improving steadily in a supportive environment.

What skills will you gain?

  • Strategic thinking and planning
  • Logical reasoning and pattern recognition
  • Focus and patience during extended gameplay
  • Problem-solving under constraints
  • Big-picture awareness and long-term planning
  • Communication and collaboration through game discussions
  • Sportsmanship and resilience

Activities we do

1. Friendly Matches
Students participate in regular chess games with peers, allowing them to practise strategies and improve through experience.

2. Paired Practice
Students work in pairs to analyse games, practise tactics, and help each other understand better moves and strategies.

3. Small-Group Challenges
Students solve chess puzzles and strategic scenarios in small groups to strengthen critical thinking and teamwork.

4. Game Analysis Discussions
Students review completed games together, discussing decisions and learning from different approaches.

5. Strategy and Tactics Practice
Students practise common chess tactics and long-term planning techniques to improve their gameplay.

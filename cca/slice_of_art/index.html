Slice of Art

Explore creativity, imagination, and artistic expression.

Get to know Slice of Art

Slice of Art is a creative CCA that encourages students to explore visual art through experimentation, collaboration, and personal expression.

The club provides a safe and inclusive environment where students can develop confidence in their artistic abilities, share ideas, and create meaningful artwork. Through guided projects and creative exploration, students engage with art beyond the classroom while building discipline, empathy, and respect for diverse perspectives.

Activity Day, Time, Venue & Advisor

Day: Once a week (two sessions may be conducted)
Time: Based on school activity schedule
Venue: Art & Design Studios / Designated Creative Spaces
Advisor: School Art Teachers

Learning Objectives

  • Develop confidence in creative expression and visual communication.
  • Explore different art media, techniques, and creative processes.
  • Build observational skills, imagination, and critical thinking.
  • Strengthen collaboration, communication, and peer support.
  • Practice reflection, discipline, and perseverance through creative challenges.
  • Develop appreciation for art, culture, and diverse perspectives.

Suitable Students & Why You Should Join

Slice of Art is open to students who are interested in art and creative exploration, regardless of prior experience.

Students who enjoy drawing, painting, experimenting with materials, or expressing ideas visually will benefit from joining this club. Members participate in creative projects, collaborative artwork, and art discussions while learning to respect shared materials and creative spaces.

The club provides a supportive environment where students can grow creatively while developing confidence, patience, and resilience.

What skills will you gain?

  • Drawing, painting, mixed media, and sculpture techniques
  • Visual observation and interpretation skills
  • Creative problem-solving and idea development
  • Understanding of visual elements and composition
  • Critical and creative thinking
  • Reflection and self-assessment skills
  • Collaboration, communication, and teamwork
  • Confidence, resilience, and patience in creative work

Activities we do

1. Studio-Based Art Sessions
Students explore different art media through guided art-making sessions and creative exercises.

2. Collaborative Art Projects
Members work together to create murals, installations, and large-scale artworks for school projects.

3. Creative Exploration Workshops
Students experiment with new techniques, materials, and creative approaches to develop artistic ideas.

4. Reflection and Sharing
Students discuss their artworks, share feedback with peers, and reflect on their creative process and learning.

Future Opportunities

Students may participate in exhibitions, showcases, or collaborative art projects within the school community. The club may also organise themed art sessions such as illustration, sculpture, mixed media, and visual storytelling.

Senior students may take on leadership roles such as Art Mentors or Exhibition Assistants and help guide younger members in creative projects.

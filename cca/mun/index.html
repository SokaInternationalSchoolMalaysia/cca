Get to know Model United Nations (MUN)

Model United Nations (MUN) is an academic simulation of the United Nations where students role-play as delegates representing different countries to discuss global issues.

Students research assigned countries and topics, debate international problems, negotiate with other delegates, and propose solutions by drafting resolutions following formal United Nations procedures.

Objectives

  • Develop students’ understanding of international relations and global affairs.
  • Enhance public speaking, debate, and diplomatic skills.
  • Promote critical thinking, research, and problem-solving.
  • Build confidence, leadership, and teamwork.
  • Encourage global citizenship and awareness of world issues.

Suitable Students & Why You Should Join

Model United Nations is open to all students who are interested in current affairs and global issues.

It is especially suitable for students who enjoy debate, public speaking, leadership, and learning about law, politics, diplomacy, journalism, or international studies.

Joining MUN helps students build confidence in expressing ideas, improve English communication skills, and develop academic writing and research abilities. It also encourages teamwork, respectful debate, and leadership development while preparing students for future academic and global opportunities.

What skills will you gain?

  • Public speaking and debate skills
  • Research and analytical thinking
  • Critical thinking and problem-solving
  • Diplomacy and negotiation skills
  • Leadership and teamwork
  • Time management and organisation
  • Confidence and professional conduct
  • Formal academic writing (position papers and resolutions)

Activities we do

1. Country and Topic Research
Students research assigned countries and global topics to understand their positions on international issues.

2. Debate and Discussion Sessions
Members participate in structured debates where they present arguments and defend their country’s perspective.

3. Resolution Writing
Students work together to draft resolutions that propose solutions to global challenges.

4. Negotiation and Diplomacy Activities
Delegates negotiate with other countries to build alliances and reach agreements during simulations.

5. Mock MUN Conferences
Students participate in simulated MUN sessions to practise formal procedures and public speaking.

Expected Outcomes

Students may have opportunities to participate in inter-school or international Model United Nations conferences.

Through these experiences, students build confidence, improve academic performance, and develop leadership qualities that prepare them to become future global citizens and school ambassadors.

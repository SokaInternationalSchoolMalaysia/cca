Music Band

Create music together and perform exciting songs as a band.

Get to know Music Band CCA

Music Band CCA is an activity where students form a band using different instruments to play songs from various genres.

Students collaborate to experiment with music, improvise together, and create exciting covers of pop songs. Members also share their favourite styles and artists, introducing one another to new songs, singers, bands, and musical styles while enjoying the creative process of making music together.

What does students do?

During sessions, students practise playing instruments together, explore different musical styles, and experiment with arranging songs as a group.

Band members collaborate to practise performances, share musical ideas, and improve their coordination as a band while developing their musical creativity and teamwork.

Suitable Students & Why You Should Join

Music Band CCA is suitable for students who have musical experience with pop instruments such as piano, guitar, drums, bass, or vocals. It is also open to students who are interested in learning these instruments as a hobby, interest, or passion.

Students who join will enjoy working alongside band mates who share the same love for music while playing both classic and modern pop songs. The club creates an energetic and exciting atmosphere where everyone can enjoy performing and creating music together.

What skills will you gain?

  • Instrument playing and ensemble performance skills
  • Teamwork and collaboration while performing as a band
  • Creativity through music improvisation and arrangement
  • Discipline, commitment, and consistent practice
  • Communication and coordination within a band
  • Knowledge of band setup and performance preparation
  • Open-mindedness towards different musical styles

Activities we do

1. Band Practice Sessions
Students practise playing songs together while improving rhythm, timing, and coordination as a group.

2. Song Arrangement and Experimentation
Members explore creative ways to arrange songs and experiment with different musical ideas and styles.

3. Performance Preparation
Students rehearse songs for school performances and events, developing stage presence and confidence.

4. Instrument Collaboration
Members learn how to combine different instruments effectively depending on the band size, venue, and style of music.
